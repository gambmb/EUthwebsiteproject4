NEW · JUNE 2026

We're coming to your city — and it's completely free.

EU'th is launching in Senica, Dunaújváros, and Galați this autumn. Free English sessions, real people, zero paperwork.

📍 Senica · Dunaújváros · Galați 🗓 Autumn 2026 🎓 Ages 15–25 💶 100% free

Maybe you've been wanting to improve your English for a while. Maybe you need it for work, for travel, or just to finally understand what people are saying in that YouTube video. Whatever the reason — EU'th is here, it's real, and it's coming to your neighbourhood.

EU'th (short for European Youth) is a community project co-funded by the Erasmus+ Programme. We run free, relaxed English sessions for young people aged 15 to 25 — led by peer facilitators, held in local spaces like schools and youth centres, and designed without the stress of grades or tests. Think of it less like a class and more like a regular meetup where the language you practise is English.

This autumn, we're opening our doors in three new cities: Senica in Slovakia, Dunaújváros in Hungary, and Galați in Romania. Each city was chosen because of its active youth scene and community spirit — and because we believe every young person deserves access to the language that opens doors across Europe, regardless of where they grow up.

Your city. Your sessions.

Senica (Slovakia)

A lively town in western Slovakia with a strong youth community and a culture of civic engagement. Our sessions here will be held in local schools and the cultural centre.

Dunaújváros (Hungary)

A city on the Danube known for its university and industrial heritage. With a young population and strong vocational education scene, it's a natural home for EU'th.

Galați (Romania)

A port city on the Danube, Galați is one of Romania's most historically rich cities. It's also home to tens of thousands of young people ready to connect with Europe.

— EU'th Project Team

Why English? And why now?

English is the shared language of Europe — the one that lets you talk to someone from Portugal, Poland, or Germany, get a job in Berlin, apply for a university in the Netherlands, or just chat with someone you met at a festival. But access to good English education isn't equal everywhere, and in many mid-sized and rural cities across Central Europe, private lessons or language schools simply aren't affordable.

That's the gap EU'th fills. Our sessions are built around speaking — real conversations, not grammar drills. You'll practise with other young people from your city, led by a peer facilitator who's been through the same experience. No textbooks. No tests. Just practice, in a space where it's okay to make mistakes.

What you actually get out of it

  • Real speaking confidence: Small groups, relaxed atmosphere. You'll speak from Day 1 — not after months of grammar study.
  • European network: Meet other EU'th participants from Slovakia, Hungary, and Romania. Make friends across borders.
  • Erasmus+ certificate: Participants who complete the programme receive a certificate backed by the EU's Erasmus+ framework — recognised across Europe.
  • Free digital materials: Access to our open learning resources online — useful outside sessions too, at your own pace.

Especially useful if you're in VET

If you're studying at a vocational school or doing an apprenticeship, English might feel less important than your technical skills. But English is becoming essential in almost every trade — whether you're working in automotive, hospitality, IT, construction, healthcare, or logistics. Here's what EU'th specifically adds for VET students:

  • Workplace English: We cover practical language for your field — communicating with clients, reading technical documents, handling international colleagues.
  • Europass-ready skills: Your participation and certificate can be added to your Europass CV — the EU standard that employers across Europe recognise instantly.
  • Opens Erasmus+ mobility: VET Erasmus+ placements abroad require basic English. EU'th helps you hit that threshold — and maybe discover a placement you wouldn't have applied for before.
  • Better job prospects: Studies show that English-speaking workers in Central Europe earn significantly more on average. A free programme can have very real economic value for you.

How to join — it takes about 3 minutes

  1. Fill in the short sign-up form

    Just your name, city, age, and a quick note about why you want to join. No academic records, no previous results needed.

  2. Get your confirmation

    We'll send you an email with the session schedule, the venue in your city, and everything you need to know before the first meetup.

  3. Show up for the first session

    Bring yourself — no textbook, no prep, no stress. Just come along, see what it's like, and meet your group.

  4. Complete the programme

    After attending the full cycle, you'll receive your official EU'th + Erasmus+ participant certificate — free of charge.

Ready to join? Spots are limited.

Sessions start this autumn in Senica, Dunaújváros, and Galați. Sign up now to secure your place — it's free, and it takes three minutes.

7 Ways English Can Open Doors for Young People

English is not just about passing exams. For many young people, it can create new choices, new friendships, and new confidence.

Here are seven ways English can open doors.

  1. It can support future jobs

    Many employers value English because it helps people communicate with customers, colleagues, and international partners. Even basic confidence can make a young person feel more prepared for interviews and applications.

  2. It can make travel less intimidating

    Travelling to another country can feel overwhelming when you are worried about being misunderstood. English can make asking for directions, reading signs, or meeting new people feel easier.

  3. It can help young people join exchanges

    European youth exchanges are built on communication. English often helps participants from different countries work together, share ideas, and build friendships.

  4. It can increase confidence online

    From tutorials to job platforms, a lot of digital content is in English. Understanding more of it can help young people learn independently and access free resources.

  5. It can make studying feel more possible

    Some students dream about studying abroad or joining an international course. English can make that dream feel less distant and more realistic.

  6. It can connect communities

    When young people from Romania, Hungary, and Slovakia meet through EU'th, English becomes a shared tool. It helps turn borders into conversations.

  7. It can help young people feel heard

    The goal is not perfect grammar. The goal is having the confidence to speak, ask, explain, and take part. Every voice matters, even while it is still learning.

This is why EU'th focuses on free, practical, and youth-led English learning. Language should not be a barrier that separates young people from opportunity. It should be a bridge.

Join EU'th for an English Cultural Event in Slovakia

This month, EU'th is bringing young people together in Slovakia for a special English cultural event designed to celebrate language, connection, and confidence.

The event will give participants the chance to explore English-speaking culture in a fun and welcoming environment. Instead of learning English only through textbooks, young people will experience it through music, games, conversation, food, traditions, and creative activities.

For EU'th, English is not just a subject. It is a bridge between people, countries, and opportunities. This event will help young people practise English in a relaxed setting where making mistakes is part of learning. The focus is not on perfect grammar, but on speaking, listening, sharing ideas, and feeling more confident.

During the event, participants will take part in interactive activities linked to English-speaking culture. These may include quizzes, team challenges, cultural presentations, short discussions, and creative workshops. Each activity is designed to help young people use English naturally while learning more about the world around them.

The event will also be a space for friendship. Young people from different backgrounds will have the chance to meet, work together, and discover how language can bring communities closer. By connecting local youth in Slovakia with wider European values, EU'th hopes to show that English can open doors to travel, education, volunteering, and future work.

Events like this are important because confidence often grows outside the classroom. When young people use English in real conversations and enjoyable activities, they begin to see the language as something useful, not something intimidating.

EU'th believes every young person deserves access to language learning, cultural exchange, and new opportunities. This English cultural event in Slovakia is one more step toward that goal.

Whether you already feel confident speaking English or are just beginning, you are welcome. Come ready to learn, meet new people, and take part in a celebration of language and culture.

Together, we are building bridges, not barriers.
