About the cause

Language access is opportunity.

EU'th responds to unequal access to quality English education in rural and lower-income communities across Central Europe.

The challenge

Talent is everywhere. Access is not.

Young people increasingly need English for employment, education, mobility and participation in European civic life. Yet geography, income and limited local provision can restrict access.

Who EU'th serves

  • Young people aged 16–25 with limited English
  • Rural and low-income communities
  • Young job seekers
  • Schools, youth centres and educators
  • Families and local employers
Social value proposition

Skills, confidence and solidarity.

Equal access

Free, quality education for young people who may otherwise be excluded.

European participation

Greater confidence to study, work, travel and take part in civic life.

Cross-cultural understanding

Peer learning and exchanges that turn borders into relationships.

Governance

Youth voices shape the organisation.

A board with youth representatives helps ensure decisions reflect the needs and experiences of beneficiaries.

EU'th commits to clear reporting to Erasmus+, public funders, partners and participating communities.

An ethics and inclusion committee supports fair access, equal opportunity and responsible programme delivery.
